Air Quality Health Effects for Particle Pollution
Levels of Concern Description of Air Quality Action
Good
(0-50) 		Air quality is good. No limitations.
Moderate
(51-100) 		Air quality is acceptable. People unusually sensitive to air pollution may begin to notice health impacts. Sensitive individuals should monitor personal health and may need to limit vigorous outdoor activities.
Unhealthy for Sensitive Groups
(101-150) 		Members of sensitive groups may experience adverse health effects. The general public is less likely to be affected. Sensitive individuals should minimize outdoor activity and retreat to an indoor clean air space if symptomatic.
Unhealthy
(151-200) 		Air pollution levels are unhealthy for all people. Health effects on sensitive populations can be serious. Everyone benefits from remaining in a clean indoor air space. Shut windows and doors. Activate indoor air filters. If outdoors, keep activities light and short in duration. Retreat to an indoor clean air space if symptomatic.
Very Unhealthy
(201-300) 		The risk of negative health effects is increased for the entire population. Everyone should minimize outdoor time. Retreat to indoor clean air spaces. Reschedule or relocate outdoor events.
Hazardous
(301+) 		Air pollution levels are harmful to all people. Anyone could experience serious health effects. Stay indoors. Remain in indoor clean air spaces. Reschedule or relocate outdoor events.
